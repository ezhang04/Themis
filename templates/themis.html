Who is Themis

In Greek mythology and religion, Themis is the goddess and personification of justice, divine order, law, and custom. She is one of the Titans, daughter of Uranus (Sky) and Gaia (Earth), and the second wife of Zeus. Themis is the mother of the Horae (Seasons) and the Moirai (Fates).

Themis is often depicted holding a pair of scales, symbolizing balance and fairness, and sometimes a sword, representing the enforcement of justice. She is also frequently portrayed blindfolded, indicating impartiality and objectivity in her judgments.

As a deity associated with law and order, Themis played a crucial role in maintaining societal harmony and was revered as a symbol of fairness and equity in ancient Greek culture.
